Critical Thinking

· 2020-05-29 · # Learning

Process: Argument+Counter Argument+Synthesis

Types: BARRIERS

  • Biases
    The writer's objectivity needs to be questioned:
    a broader ideology, cultural experience, limitations, prejudice, motivation without the basis of facts and objectivity…

  • Assumption:
    Judgement without evidence;
    Not considering other related factors.

  • Reasoning:
    Logical?
    Reasons, clear development, examples, evidence…

  • Relevance
    Are the ideas related to your work?
    Are the ideas too old?

  • Implications
    Definition: a possible effect or result of an action or a decision.
    From the perspective of your own writing: Should it be used? Can it be used? Will it contradict or affect other cited arguments? How to explain it?

  • Evidence
    Validity, gaps, representativity…

  • Response
    How has the writer's idea been received in the wider academic community?
    Have the ideas been mainly criticized or supported?
    Are the criticisms valid?

  • Situations
    The contexts that the idea applied to; the time and situation when the idea was written
